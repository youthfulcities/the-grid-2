How was the index created?

This data was collected by Youthful Cities in collaboration with The Tamarack Institute, organizations participating in the Communities Building Youth Futures Initiative, and with youth in the communities they serve. The community partners went through the following steps:

  1. Define topics
  2. Select indicators
  3. Select measurements

Where possible, existing indicators and measurements from the Pivot 2020 project were used (see the Pivot 2020 methodology). Where a need was identified, new indicators and measurements were proposed.

To review indicators and measurements the Indicator Design Committee (IDC) was assembled. This committee was composed of youth and youth organization representatives from the communities / cities involved in the data collection project. The purpose of the committee members is to engage and put forward youth perspectives on what matters most to them in their communities.

IDC members were asked the following question for each topic:

  • In your words, how would you define (add topic here)?
  • For example: In your words, how would you define Employment and Mentorship?

IDC members then completed the following questions for each measurement:

  • On a scale of 1 to 5, please rate the (add indicator name) Indicator.
  • For example: On a scale of 1 to 5, please rate the "Youth Employment Centres" Indicator.
    • How relevant is this indicator to measure Employment and Mentorship?
    • How inclusive is this indicator of the community your organization is serving/you are living in?
    • How intuitive is the language/wording of this indicator?
    • If you were to try to get data on this indicator in your city today, how easy would it be to access?

Each Indicator is also followed up with the following short answer question:

  • Reflecting on your evaluation, this indicator would be even better if:

Finally, the IDC were asked the following questions in review:

  • Now that you have reviewed all the indicators for (topic), according to your lived experience and reality, this list of indicators would be even better if we:
    • (List Indicator here):
      1. Keep it
      2. Keep with modifications
      3. Remove it
  • Aside from the above, could you think of any new indicators that would assist in measuring/collecting data for the (add topic) topic in your community?

When assessing the results, the final question in each topic was used as the base for assessment. Indicators that received support two thirds (66.6%) IDC (members who voted for “Keep it”) were passed without modifications, while the remaining were further reviewed by the IDC.

After the IDC members were able to discuss and propose modifications to indicators, the indicators were reworded or removed as needed. The measurements may just need to be reworded to meet community understanding and language, or certain ones may need to be modified to align with youth needs and collectibility. Example: “Student to career counsellor ratio in high school” would be easier to collect by simply asking "Is there a dedicated career counsellor available in high school"? The change would remove the assumption that career counselling already exists.

Final feedback was collected from IDC members after changes had been made for any further thoughts.

After indicators and measurements had been finalized, data was collected. Data collectors had strong lived experience in the cities they collected in.

Following data collection, the data was validated to ensure accuracy and completion. This includes validating sources, making sure values are in the right format, and ensuring values are comparable. Example: If the data is asking collectors to enter "annual cost of a transit pass", ensure the value entered is not cost per month. If it is, multiply it by 12 and update the value.

Once the data was validated, it was cleaned by our data analysts and uploaded to the Grid for all to use and learn from.
